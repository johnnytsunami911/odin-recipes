California Burrito

Description

California Burritos are a staple in California and they are one of my favorite meals. A big burrito filled with potatoes and carene asada always hits the spot.

After a long morning of surfing this meal is hands down the best thing to eat on an empty stomach. The smell of the ocean mixed with the beautful burrito is nostalgic.

Ingredients

  • Tortillas
  • Carne Asada
  • Shredded Cheddar Cheese
  • Lime Juice
  • French Fries
  • Avocados
  • Salsa
  • Sour Cream

Steps

  1. Whisk together ground cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, chili powder, onion powder, pepper, cayenne pepper. Add 3 tablespoons to a freezer bag and reserve the rest for later. Add olive oil, reduced sodium soy sauce, orange juice, lime juice, brown sugar and liquid smoke.
  2. Add steak and seal bag, removing excess air. Marinate in the refrigerator 4-12 hours.
  3. Add 2 avocados, sour cream, mayonnaise, lime juice, salt, cumin and garlic powder to your blender and process until smooth.
  4. Spread potatoes evenly over prepared baking sheet and bake for 20 minutes then toss, and stir and rearrange fries in a single layer and bake for an additional 10 minutes. If you like crispier fries, then broil until golden and crispy.
  5. Grill marinated/rubbed carne asada for 5-6 minutes per side, or until an internal thermometer reads 130-135 degrees F for medium rare or around 140 degrees for medium. Grill time will vary depending on thickness of your steak and desired level of doneness.
  6. To assemble, layer carne asada in center of each burrito followed by, fries, cheese, avocado crema, salsa then pineapple. Fold in top and bottom and roll tightly in burrito style.
